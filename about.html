O C E A N O S

National and Technical University of Athens

Oceanos NTUA is a non-profit, research team, consisted of undergraduate students of School of Naval Architecture and Marine Engineering, representing National Technical University of Athens in international competitions.

Hydrocontest

Years 2016-17-18

  • 200kg The Mass Transport Competition

    • Each vessel must hold 200 kg of ballast to simulate the displacement of a cargo vessel

  • 20kg The Lightweight Competition

    • The prototypes, loaded with 20 kg, represent leisure boats

  • The Long-Distance Race

    • The teams must cover the maximum distance within one hour

  • Same Battery & Motor

    • All the teams are provided with the same electric engine. The amount of the available energy is imposed to travel a set distance and the ranking is made according to the running time. The battery that is used is a 36 V with 7800 mAh.

  • Imposed Size

    • Maximum dimensions: 2.50m x 2.50m x 2.00m

Solar & Energy Boat Challenge

Years 2019-Present

  • Energy Class

    • Design the most powerful and enduring propulsion system as possible with a limited amount of energy. The types of energy that can be used are: bio fuel, battery, hydrogen, compressed air, LNG, etc. – the choice is wide, but it must be a clean source.

  • Solar Class

    • Boats in the Solar Class will undertake match racing, slalom, and endurance races

  • Design

    • Given a one-design catamaran hull, contestants need to build a cockpit for their boat according to these specifications: Length: 5m – Beam: 2.5m – Height: 0.45m – Displacement: 250kg – Speed: 15 knots.

  • Energy - Offshore Class

    • At an offshore race, boats must comply with YCM Offshore Class rules, which means being able to take three people and meet technical specifications defined in collaboration with the UIM. The two harbours are set to be interconnected soon, but meanwhile this race will serve as an important test for boats, close to commercialization, with an eco-responsible propulsion on the sea.

A Sustainable Future

Our team's aim is the contribution to the UN Sustainable Develofpement Goals. We strive to minimize our carbon footprint and reduce the impact of human activities on our planet, working to preserve our oceans and forests. Our research revolves around 6 of these Goals.
